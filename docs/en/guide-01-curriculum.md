# Study Guide 01 — Strategy and the 4-Month Plan

> Companion to lecture 01. Translates the motivation into an actual learning plan.
> Read once before you start, and revisit every Sunday for progress checks.

---

## 0. Pre-Start Self-Assessment

Answer these five questions:

1. Do you have any prior Chinese-language exposure? (Yes/No)
2. Can you guess the meaning of Sino-Korean / Sino-Japanese compounds you have not seen before? (Yes/No)
3. Can you commit at least 5 hours per week? (Yes/No)
4. Is your goal speaking, reading, or both? (Speaking / Reading / Both)
5. Are you willing to write a real GitHub Issue in Chinese after 4 months? (Yes/No)

| Pattern | Recommendation |
|---------|----------------|
| 1·2 both "Yes" | Standard 4-month plan (this guide as-is) |
| 1 "No" + 2 "Yes" | Standard 4 months. Hanzi-cognate intuition guarantees a fast start |
| 1 "Yes" + 2 "No" | Extend Month 1 to 5 weeks |
| 3 "No" | Extend to 6 or 8 months |
| 4 "Speaking" | Wrong curriculum. Use HelloChinese or a conversation-first program |
| 5 "No" | The capstone is the engine of motivation. Reconsider |

**English speakers without Hanzi background:** plan on 6–8 months. Double the Month 1 Hanzi target and add 4 weeks between Month 1 and Month 2 for a "Hanzi consolidation" phase.

## 1. The 4-Month Plan

### Month 1 — Pinyin · Hanzi · Grammar Skeleton

| Week | Output |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Pinyin basics + tone recognition + 50 radicals |
| 2 | 100 Hanzi (vocab categories 1–2) + 5 grammar patterns |
| 3 | 200 Hanzi cumulative (categories 3–4) + 10 grammar patterns |
| 4 | 300 Hanzi cumulative + 15 grammar patterns + first model-card paragraph attempt |

### Month 2 — Tech Doc Reading

| Week | Output |
|------|--------|
| 5 | 1 ModelScope model card (Qwen series) |
| 6 | 2 model cards cumulative (DeepSeek + ChatGLM) |
| 7 | 5 cards cumulative + 3 paper abstracts |
| 8 | 10 paper abstracts cumulative |

### Month 3 — Community Reading

| Week | Output |
|------|--------|
| 9 | 1 Zhihu troubleshooting thread (vLLM OOM type) |
| 10 | 3 Zhihu answers cumulative |
| 11 | 2 CSDN technical posts |
| 12 | 1,000 Hanzi cumulative + 400 domain words |

### Month 4 — Writing & Shipping

| Week | Output |
|------|--------|
| 13 | 3 draft GitHub Issues (not yet posted) |
| 14 | 1 real GitHub Issue posted (Chinese OSS project) |
| 15 | Zhihu answer draft (300+ characters) |
| 16 | Zhihu answer posted + graduation |

## 2. Daily and Weekend Routine

### Weekday 45 min

```
00:00 ~ 00:10  Anki Hanzi/vocab — 30 cards (new + review)
00:10 ~ 00:35  Read one paragraph of real material (model card / Zhihu / CSDN)
00:35 ~ 00:45  Log unknown words + queue for Anki + DeepL comparison
```

### Weekend 90 min

```
00:00 ~ 00:30  Anki review — clear backlog
00:30 ~ 01:15  Read one full page (longer-form material)
01:15 ~ 01:30  Weekly retrospective: hit rate, stuck patterns, next-week tweak
```

## 5. Measurement and Feedback

**Daily**
- Anki hit rate (target 80%+)
- Time to read one paragraph (Week 1: ~30 min → Week 16: ~5 min)

**Weekly (Sunday)**
- Compare your reading vs. DeepL — divergences are learning targets
- Hit rate below 70% → reduce new cards, double down on review

**Monthly**
- Can you read a fresh 1-page document in 30 min without DeepL?
- If no: revisit Month 1 vocabulary

---

## 6. Staying Motivated

The slump usually hits in Weeks 5–6 (early Month 2). Reason: you have memorized your domain vocabulary, but suddenly the unknown words explode in real material.

Three responses:
1. **Switch to material from a domain you love.** Stellaris Chinese wiki, Chinese Magic-the-Gathering articles, anything. Increase Hanzi exposure outside the technical register.
2. **Re-anchor on the goal.** One GitHub Issue posted in Chinese after 4 months. That single artifact is graduation.
3. **Re-read your old paragraphs.** A paragraph that took 30 minutes in Week 4 will take 5 minutes in Week 8. The delta is your evidence.

---

## 7. Graduation Outputs

**Output 1: One real GitHub Issue.** Choose a Chinese OSS project (vLLM, ms-swift, FastChat, ChatGLM, etc.) and post a real issue. Template: `docs/en/github-issue-template.md`. You do not need a maintainer reply — the act of writing and posting is the deliverable.

**Output 2: One Zhihu answer (300+ characters).** Find a question in your domain, write the answer, post it. The first one will be awkward. Posting it anyway is what graduates you.

---

## 8. After Graduation

1. **Daily reading habit** — one model-card paragraph or one Zhihu answer per day
2. **Second OSS contribution** — escalate to a PR (code + Chinese PR description)
3. **Domain expansion** — MLOps → autonomous driving, robotics, multimodal
4. **Optional: Japanese** — leverage your Hanzi assets

## FAQ

**Q. Tones — do I really not need to pronounce them correctly?**
A. Correct. You only need to recognize them so you can type pinyin into Pleco.

**Q. What about the HSK exam?**
A. This curriculum is not HSK prep. HSK weights speaking and listening heavily; you need a separate program.

**Q. Will I be able to converse after 4 months?**
A. No. Speaking is intentionally excluded. Conversation needs a separate 6–12 month investment.

**Q. 45 minutes a day is too much.**
A. 30 minutes works. Graduation timeline becomes 5–6 months. The only failure mode is zero minutes.

**Q. What exactly will I be able to read?**
A. ModelScope / Hugging Face model cards as a primary source, Zhihu troubleshooting answers fully, and you will be able to write GitHub Issues in Chinese. Full academic papers (not abstracts) require additional study after graduation.
